# BG_CHAPTER_ANALYSIS_FRAMEWORK.md
Bhagavad-Gītā Chapter Analysis Framework

Track A — Chapter-Level Analytical Layer

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines a **structured framework for analyzing each chapter of the Bhagavad-gītā**.

Chapter-level analysis enables:

• understanding philosophical progression  
• curriculum design  
• educational summaries  
• thematic navigation  
• knowledge graph clustering  

While verses form the **atomic units**, chapters form the **philosophical modules** of the Gītā.

---

# 2. Why Chapter Analysis Matters

The Bhagavad-gītā unfolds as a **dialogue with progressive teaching**.

Each chapter:

• introduces philosophical themes  
• develops certain yogic disciplines  
• answers earlier questions  
• prepares the reader for later teachings  

Understanding this progression is essential.

---

# 3. Chapter Analysis Structure

Each chapter should be analyzed using a consistent structure.

Chapter Overview Context in Dialogue Philosophical Themes Concept Clusters Key Verses Commentary Emphasis Knowledge Graph Role Educational Applications
---

# 4. Chapter Overview

Basic chapter information.

Example structure:

Chapter Number: 2 Chapter Title: Sāṅkhya Yoga Verses: 72
Additional description:

A brief summary explaining the overall subject of the chapter.

---

# 5. Dialogue Context

Explain the narrative context of the chapter within the Mahābhārata dialogue.

Example:

Arjuna collapses in despair. Kṛṣṇa begins philosophical instruction. The nature of the self is introduced.
This step preserves the **dramatic structure of the Gītā**.

---

# 6. Philosophical Themes

Identify the major themes discussed in the chapter.

Example:

nature_of_self karma detachment equanimity yoga
Themes should align with concepts defined in the **concept registry**.

---

# 7. Concept Clusters

Identify clusters of concepts that dominate the chapter.

Example for Chapter 2:

atman self_realization karma detachment equanimity buddhi_yoga
These clusters help build **chapter-level knowledge graph nodes**.

---

# 8. Key Verses

Some verses function as **philosophical anchors**.

Example from Chapter 2:

bg.2.11 bg.2.13 bg.2.20 bg.2.47 bg.2.55
These verses often appear in commentarial discussions.

---

# 9. Concept Distribution

Analyze which concepts appear most frequently in the chapter.

Example:

karma → 8 verses atman → 6 verses detachment → 10 verses
This allows visualizations such as **concept density maps**.

---

# 10. Commentary Emphasis

Different commentators may emphasize different aspects of the chapter.

Example:

Śaṅkara: focus on knowledge of the self
Rāmānuja: focus on devotion through duty
Madhva: focus on proper performance of action
Śrīla Prabhupāda: focus on devotional service
This helps build **commentary comparison layers**.

---

# 11. Philosophical Role in the Gītā

Explain the chapter's role in the philosophical progression.

Example:

Chapter 2 introduces the fundamental metaphysical teaching of the self. It establishes the philosophical basis for later teachings on yoga and action.
This explains **how the Gītā develops its argument**.

---

# 12. Relationship to Other Chapters

Identify connections with other chapters.

Example:

Chapter 2 → introduces karma yoga Chapter 3 → elaborates karma yoga Chapter 5 → clarifies renunciation and action
These links strengthen the **chapter-level knowledge graph**.

---

# 13. Educational Applications

Chapters often serve as units for teaching.

Example:

Course Module: Introduction to Karma Yoga
Lessons: Self and body distinction Action and detachment Equanimity
This supports the **Education pillar**.

---

# 14. Guidance Themes

Some chapters contain teachings applicable to real-life situations.

Example:

decision making ethical duty overcoming despair self discipline
This supports the **Guidance pillar**.

---

# 15. Chapter-Level Knowledge Graph Node

Each chapter may function as a node in the knowledge graph.

Example:

Chapter 2 ↓ atman karma detachment equanimity
This enables chapter-level navigation.

The same framework may be applied to other texts:

# 20. Final Principle

Verses reveal individual teachings.

Chapters reveal philosophical structure.

Understanding both levels allows the platform to present the Bhagavad-gītā as a **coherent philosophical dialogue rather than isolated verses**.
